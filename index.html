About

Electroverse

Thadomal Shahani Engineering College (TSEC) is a prominent institution in Mumbai, housing Electroverse.Comm-TSEC, a Technical Committee associated with the Electronics and Telecommunication Department. This committee orchestrates TECH-A-THON, a 48-hour Hardware Hackathon, offering students a platform to showcase innovative solutions to real-world problems with a technical edge. The event, promoting collaboration and creativity, encourages participants to engage in the Vortex of Technovation, fostering a culture of exploration and excellence within the TSEC community. TECH-A-THON not only serves as a competition but also facilitates learning, networking, and collaboration among students, industry professionals, and experts. The hackathon aligns with TSEC's commitment to holistic education, empowering students to be innovative leaders in the evolving realm of engineering and technology.

About

College

Thadomal Shahani Engineering College (TSEC) is a private engineering college located in Mumbai, India, established in 1983 by the Hyderabad (Sind) National Collegiate Board (HSNC Board). It is the first and oldest private engineering institute affiliated with the University of Mumbai. Approved by the Directorate of Technical Education of Maharashtra in 1983, TSEC is one of India's oldest private engineering colleges. It pioneered undergraduate-level studies in fields such as computer engineering, information technology, and biomedical engineering. The Department of Biomedical Engineering, founded in 1983, is one of the oldest in India. TSEC introduced Computer Engineering in 1988, Information Technology in 1998, and later established departments like Electronics & Telecommunication Engineering, Chemical Engineering (1983), and Biotechnology (2004). In 2020, the college initiated an undergraduate course in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science. The college is named after Thadomal Shahani, a respected philanthropist in Mumbai.

About

Department

Department of Electronics and Telecommunication was established in year 2002 with a clear view of a forerunner in the field of Telecommunications, the Department seeks to evolve towards excellence and adapt itself to the rapid advancements in specialized communication related fields. This Department earlier had nomenclature of Electronics Engineering from 1983 to 2001. From 2002 Student intake for E&TC has been 60 students. At the desktop of achievements of the department, lies the award of accreditation given by the National Board of Accreditation since 2008. The Department is accredited by NBA continuously for the past 4 cycles and recently by NAAC. The department has also started a PhD program, with an intake of 10 students from the academic year 2014-15. The curriculum of the program has been designed to cater to the ever changing needs and demands of communication industry. The focus is on holistic learning and empowering students to make significant contributions at all levels. The open elective system gives a handful of choices for students to develop expertise in their area of interest such as Neural Networks, Radar engineering, VLSI, Data compression and encryption, Satellite communication, Image processing, Artificial Intelligence, and Machine Learning etc. It has one of the best state-of-the-art hard ware and software laboratories with special focus on telecommunication.

Frequently asked questions

How can I participate in TECH-A-THON?

To participate, you need to register on Electroverse.Comm's website during the registration period. Keep an eye on the official communication channels for announcements regarding registration dates and requirements. Space is limited, so early registration is recommended.

Are there any prerequisites for participation?

While there are no strict prerequisites, participants are encouraged to have a basic understanding of hardware development, programming, and problem-solving. Teams with diverse skill sets often perform well. Bring your own hardware tools and equipment, as the event will provide a platform for hands-on experimentation.

When and where will TECH-A-THON take place?

TECH-A-THON will be held offline for 48 hours at a designated venue. The date, time, and location will be communicated to registered participants well in advance. Make sure to stay tuned for updates and announcements on Electroverse.Comm's official channels.

What are the judging criteria and prizes for TECH-A-THON?

Projects will be evaluated based on innovation, functionality, feasibility, and presentation. A panel of expert judges will assess the projects, and winners will be announced at the conclusion of the event. Exciting prizes, including cash awards, mentorship opportunities, and recognition in the tech community, await the top-performing teams. Stay motivated, collaborate effectively, and showcase your creativity to maximize your chances of success!
